FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How does MyDNAPedia work?
It starts with a simple cheek swab you do at home — no blood, no needles, no clinic visit. Your kit is delivered to your door with everything you need inside; you swab the inside of your cheek for about two minutes, then post it back in the prepaid envelope included in the box. Your DNA is analysed in our EU-certified laboratory, and before you ever see a single number, a qualified genetic counsellor walks you through your results one-on-one — translating raw genetic data into clear, practical guidance you can actually act on.
How accurate are the results?
Every sample is processed in our EU-certified laboratory using ASPCR (allele-specific PCR) technology, with 99.9% accuracy. MyDNAPedia was founded in 2012 and has since mapped the DNA of more than 1.8 million people, so the science behind each report is well established and rigorously validated. And because your genetic code doesn't change over time, a single test stays accurate and relevant for the rest of your life.
How long does it take to get my report?
Your results are typically delivered within 3 to 4 weeks from the day your sample reaches the lab. Once they're ready, a genetic counsellor takes you through the findings, and the full report is yours to keep — to revisit whenever you like, to share with your doctor, and to act on. Since your DNA stays the same throughout your life, that one report keeps paying off for years across nutrition, fitness, disease risk and more.
Which plan should I choose?
Every plan begins with the same simple cheek swab — you just choose how deep you want to go. Premium (MDP 360°) is the complete picture: 100+ traits spanning disease risk, nutrition, skin and hair, mental wellbeing, women's and men's health, and pharmacogenomics — how your body responds to the medicines your doctor prescribes. Standard explores one area in full depth, such as Fitness, Cardiac Health or Skin Health. Lite answers a single focused question, like weight management, nutrition, alcohol response or Alzheimer's risk. Not sure where to start? Your genetic counsellor can help you pick the right plan for your goals.
